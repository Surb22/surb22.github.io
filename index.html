Job Trends (2012-2017)

By: Surbhi Bhargava

Introduction

The New York region is divided into 10 economic regions which are:

  • Western NY
  • Finger Lakes
  • Southern Tier
  • Central NY
  • Mohawk Valley
  • Capital Region
  • Mid-Hudson
  • New York City
  • Long Island
  • North Country

Western New York : Key Industries are Biotech and Life Sciences, Agribusiness, Software and Digital Media and Tourism

Finger Lakes : Key Industries are Optics and Imaging, Biotech and Life Sciences, Software and Digital Media, and Agribusiness

Southern-Tier : Key Industries are Cybersecurity, Distribution, Financial Services and Agribusiness

Central New York : Key Industries are Cybersecurity, Life Sciences, Renewable Energy and Agribusiness

Mohawk Valley : Key Industries are Distribution, Cybersecurity, Material Processing and Electronics

Capital Region : Key Industries are Electronics, Digital Game Development, Material Processing and Renewable Energy

Mid-Hudson : Key Industries are Biotech and Life Sciences, Agribusiness, Craft Beverage and Tourism

New York City : Key Industries are Biotech and Life Sciences, Financial Services, Digital Media and Tourism

New York City : Key Industries are Biotech and Life Sciences, Financial Services, Digital Media and Tourism

Long Island : Key Industries are Biotech and Life Sciences, Renewable Energy, Craft Beverage and Tourism

North Country : Key Industries are Renewable Energy, Agribusiness, Tourism and Transporrtation Equipment

The North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) is the standard used by Federal statistical agencies in classifying business establishments for the purpose of collecting, analyzing, and publishing statistical data related to the U.S. business economy. There are 22 core industries:
  • Educational Services
  • Arts, Entertainment, and Recreation
  • Manufacturing
  • Wholesale Trade
  • Health Care and Social Assistance
  • Construction
  • Agriculture, Forestry, Fishing and Hunting
  • Unclassified Industry
  • Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services
  • Information
  • Accommodation and Food Services
  • Retail Trade
  • Administrative and Support and Waste Management and Remediation Services
  • Government
  • Management of Companies and Enterprises
  • Real Estate and Rental and Leasing
  • Mining, Quarrying, and Oil and Gas Extraction
  • Utilities
  • Finance and Insurance
  • Transportation and Warehousing
  • Other Services (except Public Administration)
